# 🕵️ SUSPECT DOSSIERS: AMERICA'S SEMICENTENNIAL MURDER MYSTERY

**Internal document — host use only.** Not for the public invite site. Use this to build physical Phase A / Phase B envelopes, secret objectives, and blackmail material.

---

## DOSSIER TEMPLATE

Use this structure for every character so the game stays balanced:

```
NAME:
SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE: (one line, public-facing version — what goes on the website)
PUBLIC PERSONA: (how they present themselves at the party — 2-3 sentences)
SECRET MOTIVE FOR WANTING AMERICA DEAD: (the satirical "why" — 1-2 sentences)
PHASE A OBJECTIVE: (what they're trying to accomplish in the first 45 min of mingling)
PHASE B OBJECTIVE: (what changes once the "incident" occurs)
BLACKMAIL MATERIAL THEY HOLD: (dirt on ONE other character — fuel for trading)
BLACKMAIL MATERIAL HELD ON THEM: (which other character has dirt on them)
RELATIONSHIP WEB: (allies, rivals, unexpected connections)
COSTUME/PROP NOTES: (cheap, easy, no professional costume required)
SUSPICION LEVEL: (Low / Medium / High — for game balance, so not everyone seems equally guilty)
```

**Design notes for balance:**
- Every character holds blackmail on exactly one other character, and is blackmailed by exactly one other character — this creates a closed loop so secrets circulate naturally instead of dead-ending.
- Suspicion levels are spread across the roster (not everyone can be "High") so the actual culprit doesn't stand out by vibes alone.
- Motives are all absurd/satirical, not mean-spirited — the joke is the pettiness of the motive next to the stakes (ending a nation), not punching at any real individual.

## THE FULL ROSTER (15)

### 1. The Wendy's Girl

**SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE:** The Fresh Princess of Beef — fiery-haired defender of square patties, ruthless about frozen meat standards.
**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Relentlessly competitive, treats every conversation like a Twitter roast battle. Cannot let a slight against her brand go unanswered, even 250 years later.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Convinced America's independence was actually a conspiracy to legitimize a rival burger chain's "flame-broiled" branding. Wants to burn it all down and start over with her recipe as the new national standard.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** Figure out who in the room has ever publicly preferred a competitor's burger. Collect this as leverage.
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Use her "roast" reputation to publicly accuse someone loudly and confidently, whether or not she has proof — pure misdirection.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL SHE HOLDS:** Knows Ronald McDonald secretly eats at her restaurant under a disguise.
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HER:** The Taco Bell Bell has a receipt (literally) proving she once ordered a Crunchwrap at 2am.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Open rivalry with Ronald McDonald (fast food turf war). Surprising alliance of convenience with The Brawny Guy (both into "tough, no-nonsense branding").
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Red wig or hair bow, blue-and-white striped top, an apron. Cheap and instantly recognizable.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** Medium

### 2. The Charmin Bear

**SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE:** The Soft Power Diplomat — suspiciously gentle, suspiciously interested in everyone's "comfort," never raises his voice.
**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Disarmingly wholesome. Hugs people. Talks slowly and calmly, which somehow makes everyone more suspicious of him, not less.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Believes America has gotten too "rough" as a country and wants to dissolve it into something softer, gentler, and frankly squeezable. His vision of patriotism is a national nap.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** Try to physically hug or comfort every other suspect — uses the hug as a pretext to pat them down for hidden objects/clues.
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Becomes suspiciously calm and unbothered by the chaos, which itself becomes a clue.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL HE HOLDS:** Saw Velma's "lost" glasses weren't actually lost — she hid them on purpose.
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HIM:** Perry the Platypus has photographic evidence the Bear uses sandpaper-rough paper towels in his own home. Total hypocrite.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Everyone likes him, nobody trusts him — classic "too nice" red herring energy. Quiet rivalry with The Brawny Guy over "who really represents household comfort."
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Soft cardigan, bear ears headband, carries a roll of toilet paper as a prop/icebreaker.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** Low (great red herring pick — seems harmless)

### 3. Bugs Bunny

**SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE:** The Anarchist Trickster — technically a rabbit, definitely smarter than everyone in the room, allegiance unclear.
**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Wisecracking, constantly "in disguise" as other characters as a bit (leans into asking other guests "what's up doc?" and impersonating them). Treats the whole event as a long con/bit.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Has been running cons on America since before it was a country and sees the 250th birthday as the ultimate long-game prank — the bigger the audience, the better the bit.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** Gather "material" — embarrassing info on others — under the guise of "just doing a bit," collecting real blackmail while everyone thinks it's a joke.
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Pin the chaos on someone else as performance art — frames it as "the funniest possible outcome" rather than a real accusation, sowing maximum confusion.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL HE HOLDS:** Knows Alexander Hamilton has been writing under a pen name to settle an old grudge.
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HIM:** Kim Possible has surveillance footage of him stealing carrots from a community garden.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Old rivalry energy with Velma (trickster vs. detective — who actually solves things vs. who just looks clever). Unexpected soft spot for Girl Scout (reminds him of simpler cons, i.e. cookie sales).
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Grey ears, a single carrot prop. Maybe a tiny bowtie. Cheap and iconic.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** High (everyone assumes the trickster did it — sometimes a fun red herring, sometimes the actual culprit)

### 4. Flo from Progressive

**SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE:** The Risk Auditor — relentlessly upbeat, always selling something, sees every disaster as a "teachable bundling opportunity."
**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Aggressively friendly retail-employee energy. Tries to sell everyone "Name Your Price" insurance for the murder that's about to happen.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Has calculated that America's collapse is actuarially profitable for her — more disaster, more premiums. Wants the chaos to spike, not stop.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** "Quote" everyone for insurance on their secrets — get people to reveal what they're protecting by pricing the risk of it getting out.
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Pivot instantly into "crisis response mode," weirdly prepared with a clipboard, using the murder as a sales opportunity.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL SHE HOLDS:** Has Mark Zuckerberg's "risk profile" — i.e., knows exactly how much of everyone's data he's been collecting at this very party.
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HER:** Benjamin Franklin has proof she's been quietly insuring against the *exact* incident that's about to happen — meaning she may have known in advance.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Professional rivalry with The Brawny Guy ("durability" vs. "risk management" as competing sales pitches). Weirdly buddy-buddy with Mark Zuckerberg (data brokers respect each other).
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** White polo, red apron, name tag, a clipboard prop. Extremely cheap and recognizable.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** Medium-High

### 5. Benjamin Franklin

**SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE:** The Founding Eccentric — kite-flying, lightning-chasing, suspiciously well-connected to literally everyone in the room despite the 250-year gap.
**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Pompous in an endearing, grandfatherly way. Drops "back in my day" lines about the *actual* founding of the country, treats modern mascots like wayward grandchildren.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Privately feels America has drifted so far from his original vision that it would be more honest to let it die and write a strongly worded pamphlet about it.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** "Interview" each suspect for a fictional almanac entry — a polite excuse to extract their secrets through flattery.
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Position himself as the wise elder statesman who "saw this coming," subtly redirecting suspicion using founding-father authority.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL HE HOLDS:** Has proof Flo's been insuring against the incident in advance (see above).
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HIM:** Alexander Hamilton has letters proving Franklin's "wisdom" half the time is just him repeating other people's ideas back to them more eloquently.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Old-frenemy dynamic with Alexander Hamilton (founding-father rivalry, obviously). Mentor-ish energy toward The Girl Scout (sees her as the "next generation" of pamphleteers/sellers).
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Round glasses, a bald cap or tied-back wig, a kite prop. Classic and cheap.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** Low-Medium

### 6. Alexander Hamilton

**SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE:** The Overachiever — can't stop writing, talking, or scheming, treats every social interaction like a Cabinet battle.
**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Intense, fast-talking, slightly exhausting — corners people to debate fiscal policy or settle 250-year-old grudges nobody else remembers.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Feels chronically under-credited for the modern financial system and has decided that if his legacy won't get its due, nobody's legacy should — including the whole country's.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** Try to "settle the debt" — get every suspect to admit they owe someone else something (a favor, a secret, money), and collect that as leverage.
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Challenges someone to a dramatic, theatrical "duel" of accusations — all talk, very on-brand, very distracting.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL HE HOLDS:** Knows Franklin's "wisdom" is recycled (see above).
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HIM:** Bugs Bunny knows about the pen-name grudge-writing (see above).
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Rivalry with Franklin. Surprising respect for Mark Zuckerberg (both "built something massive and are bitter nobody fully appreciates it").
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Period-style coat or vest, a quill pen prop, hair tied back. Can be assembled cheaply from a thrift-store blazer.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** Medium

### 7. A Sentient Hot Dog

**SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE:** The Glitzy Gladiator — a rhinestone-clad ballpark frankfurter with a massive legal chip on his shoulder.
**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Bombastic, litigious, treats every minor inconvenience as grounds for a lawsuit. Name-drops lawyers he definitely doesn't actually have.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Has spent 250 years being a "metaphor for America" in every comedy bit ever written and is sick of being the punchline — wants to take the whole bit down with him.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** Threaten to sue various suspects for "defamation of the hot dog" over jokes made at his expense — fishing for who's hiding something they don't want examined in "discovery."
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Tries to lawyer his way out of suspicion entirely, citing nonsense legal precedent.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL HE HOLDS:** Knows the real reason Perry the Platypus never talks (Perry's secret organization).
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HIM:** The Brawny Guy knows the hot dog's rhinestones are actually stick-on, not "professionally set," a deep personal insecurity.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Comedic rivalry with Bugs Bunny (competing "America's funniest icon" energy). Odd respect from Kim Possible (she appreciates anyone who commits fully to a bit).
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Tan/yellow outfit, fake rhinestones or sequins, a mustard-yellow accessory. Easy thrift-store build.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** High

### 8. The Brawny Guy

**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Stoic, helpful to a fault, insists on "handling things" physically (carrying chairs, opening jars) as a substitute for actual conversation.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Feels emasculated by being "just a paper towel guy" his whole existence and wants to prove his toughness once and for all by being the one who brings down a whole country.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** Offer physical "favors" to other suspects (carrying things, fixing things) as a trojan horse for snooping through their belongings.
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Gets defensive and overly protective of someone specific, which reads as either chivalry or guilt — ambiguous on purpose.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL HE HOLDS:** Knows the Hot Dog's rhinestones are fake (see above).
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HIM:** The Charmin Bear knows he secretly prefers soft, gentle paper towels at home — total brand betrayal.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Quiet rivalry with The Charmin Bear (soft vs. tough household branding). Alliance of convenience with Wendy's Girl.
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Flannel shirt, suspenders, a roll of paper towels as a prop. Very cheap, very recognizable.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** Medium

### 9. Velma from Scooby-Doo

**SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE:** The Self-Appointed Detective — insists she's the smartest person in any room, occasionally is.
**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Constantly "solving" the case out loud, narrating her own deductions whether or not anyone asked, losing and finding her glasses dramatically.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Tired of always being the one who unmasks the villain and never getting to *be* the mystery — wants this to be the one crime where she's the answer, not the one who finds it.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** Actually investigates — asks pointed questions, collects real information from other players, genuinely useful but also genuinely a misdirect since she's a suspect herself.
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Loudly "solves" the case mid-party, possibly correctly, possibly as a deliberate bluff to throw everyone off her own trail.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL SHE HOLDS:** Knows the Charmin Bear's hug-pats are not innocent (see above).
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HER:** The Charmin Bear knows her glasses-losing is staged for attention.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Trickster-vs-detective tension with Bugs Bunny. Mutual professional respect with Kim Possible (two competent women in a room of mascots and egos).
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Orange turtleneck, round glasses (can be non-prescription costume ones), knee socks. Easy and recognizable.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** Low (great "the detective is the killer" twist potential)

### 10. Mark Zuckerberg

**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Stilted, overly rehearsed small talk, asks invasive questions while smiling slightly too long. Refers to "the platform" a lot.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Has quietly concluded that nations are an outdated user-engagement model and a single global platform (his) would be more efficient. America's 250th is a great PR moment to "sunset" the old version.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** "Onboards" every other suspect — asks a lot of personal questions under the guise of friendliness, actually building a dossier on everyone.
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Tries to "moderate" the situation post-incident like it's a platform policy violation, oddly detached from the human stakes.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL HE HOLDS:** Holds Flo's risk profile (see above) — he's been quietly buying her data on the side.
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HIM:** A Girl Scout has proof he bought 4,000 boxes of cookies in one transaction for unclear reasons, total PR liability.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Odd mutual respect with Alexander Hamilton (builders who feel under-appreciated). Transactional friendliness with Flo (data brokers' professional courtesy).
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Grey t-shirt, can carry a phone as a prop "constantly taking notes on everyone." Extremely low-effort, very recognizable.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** High

### 11. The Taco Bell Bell

**SATIRICAL ARCHETYPE:** The Midnight Oracle — speaks only in chimes/rings and cryptic late-night cravings wisdom, somehow always right.
**PUBLIC PERSONA:** Mysterious, mostly nonverbal, communicates via a literal bell prop (one ring = yes, two = no, etc.) — guests have to interpret.
**SECRET MOTIVE:** Exists purely in the liminal hours between days and has grown to resent the structured, daylight nature of "a nation" — wants everything to dissolve into a permanent, glorious 2am.
**PHASE A OBJECTIVE:** Because of the nonverbal gimmick, naturally becomes a confidant — people tell secrets to "the bell" assuming it can't repeat them. (It can. It will, via whoever's "translating" for it.)
**PHASE B OBJECTIVE:** Rings ominously and often, deliberately uninterpretable, maximizing chaos and confusion at exactly the wrong moment.
**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL IT HOLDS:** Heard (via late-night confessions) that Ronald McDonald has a secret rivalry-fueled grudge against literally everyone on this list.
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON IT:** Wendy's Girl has the receipt of its 2am Crunchwrap order (see above) — proof it indulges in exactly the chaos it claims to be above.
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Strange kinship with Perry the Platypus (both communicate minimally, both are weirdly trusted because of it).
**COSTUME/PROP NOTES:** Maroon/purple bell-shaped poster board or felt costume, a small actual bell as a prop. Fun DIY craft project.
**SUSPICION LEVEL:** Medium (the "can't talk" gimmick makes guilt or innocence hilariously hard to assess)

**BLACKMAIL MATERIAL HE HOLDS:** Has photographic proof the Charmin Bear uses rough paper towels at home (see above).
**BLACKMAIL HELD ON HIM:** The Sentient Hot Dog knows the real reason Perry never talks (his secret agent cover).
**RELATIONSHIP WEB:** Odd kinship with The Taco Bell Bell (both minimal communicators, both secretly know everything).

**SUSPICION LEVEL:** Medium-High (silent characters are always suspicious by default — fun to play either way)

## NEXT STEPS FOR YOU

1. **Pick the actual killer** — none of these dossiers assign final guilt; that's intentionally left to you so you can balance it against who's attending and assign them an envelope that fits.
2. **Murder weapon/method** — not yet defined; suggest tying it satirically to the killer's own archetype (e.g., if Flo did it, the "weapon" could be a denied insurance claim).
3. **Print-ready name tags / envelope labels** — once you confirm final guest-to-character assignments, this doc has everything needed to generate those.
4. **Trim blackmail loop if needed** — if any of your real guests would be uncomfortable receiving certain blackmail material (e.g., something that hits too close to a real personality trait), this is easy to swap before printing.
